Research

My research focuses on microeconomic theory, industrial organisation and experimental economics. Particularly, I am interested in horizontal and vertical product differentiations and decision theories under risk.My PhD dissertation project is supervised by Dr Subir Bose (University of Leicester), Dr Jacob Seifert (University of Leicester) and Prof Piercarlo Zanchettin (University of Nottingham).

Working paper

  • First-order stochastic dominance, framing effects and risk preferences (experiment)

    • This paper experimentally investigates the importance of framing effects in first-order stochastic dominance (FSD) criterion violations. We find that individuals violate the FSD criterion, but these violations are associated with the underlying frame of lotteries. If the underlying frame makes the FSD superior lottery hard to identify, participants violate the FSD criterion more frequently and statistically significant. In addition, participants' sensitivity to the lottery frames is related to their risk behaviour and attitude. However, if the same choice sets are re-framed in a certain way, which makes the FSD superior lotteries easier to identify, participants reverse their decisions and select the FSD superior lotteries almost all the time, irrespective of their risk behaviour and attitude.
