Research

My research focuses on microeconomic theory, industrial organisation and experimental economics. Particularly, I am interested in horizontal and vertical product differentiations and decision theories under risk.My PhD dissertation project is supervised by Dr Subir Bose (University of Leicester), Dr Jacob Seifert (University of Leicester) and Prof Piercarlo Zanchettin (University of Nottingham).

Working paper

  • First-order stochastic dominance, framing effects and risk preferences (experiment)

    • This paper experimentally investigates the importance of framing effects in first-order stochastic dominance (FSD) criterion violations and finds that individuals violate the FSD criterion, depending on the underlying frame of lotteries. Thus, if the underlying frame makes the FSD superior lottery hard to identify, participants tend to violate the FSD criterion significantly. In this case, participantsâ€™ sensitivity to the framing effects is related to their risk preferences that play an essential role in identifying which type of them prominently violate the FSD criterion. On the contrary, if the subtle frame of lotteries makes the FSD superior lottery easy to identify, participants hardly violate the FSD criterion. Therefore, the role of risk preferences becomes negligible.
